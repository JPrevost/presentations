Your Front Page Content is great right?

I don't thinks ours sucks more than most, but a lot of it is not actually used.

Google Analytics Events

  • July 2nd: started tracking all interaction with the front page of the site.
  • Both categories of clicks and individual links clicked are tracked.
  • Not a lot of data yet, but initital trends are interesting.

Category Data

  • Popular Links: 58.83%
  • Account: 28.05%
  • Top Nav: 6.48%
  • Global Header: 3.13%
  • Popular Drop Down: 1.00%
  • News Promos: 0.81%
  • Mobile Account Nav: 0.65%
  • Popular Pages: 0.60%
  • Header: 0.32%
  • News Lists: 0.14%

83%

...is the percent of our core space (non-header/footer) that results in less than 1% of our front page clicks.

To be fair, the top half of the right column (16.67% of the width) contains no links. But still! 83% of the space results in 1% of clicks.

87%

... is the percent of our clicks that come from 16% of our horizontal space.

Or about 16% of our core space generates 87% of our clicks.

This includes Popular Links, Account (which includes lists) and Popular pages (which themselves are rarely used).

Further Breakdown Shows Continued Trend

All Popular Links: 58% of clicks

Of that...

  • PubMed: 62.16%
  • UpToDate: 20.99%
  • Ovid MEDLINE: 5.05%
  • Top Databases: 3.40%
  • Ebook Collections: 2.95%
  • MD Consult: 2.47%
  • Clinical Tools: 2.03%
  • Catalog: 0.71%
  • Mobile Resources: 0.24%
PubMed alone, a single text link, generated over 26% of our clicks. The display order is not indicative of actual popularity.

Summary

I can see two obvious take aways...

The link to PubMed should take up 25% of our core space.

Or, the link to PubMed should be even smaller so people click on other stuff.

Summary (more seriously)

While numbers don't lie, they just tell us what's wrong, not how to fix it.

  • Library news promos that take up 67% of the core space but were clicked less than 1% of the time should go away.
    • Maybe one row and then rely on the list for more news.
    • (although just a link to News might be even more appropriate)
  • What links are NOT on our front page that users actually would love to click if only they were there?
    • If we figure that out, I'll let you know.
